Greta Gerwig / Noah Baumbach
Frances Ha
2013
RACHEL
Andy and Janelle just got back from Paris.

JANELLE
Six and half hours on a plane with a baby. Won’t be doing that again soon! But it was heaven.

ANDY (to Frances)
Do you ever get to Paris?

FRANCES
Not really. No. Kind of once. Actually, no. What’s that museum with the escalators in tubes?

ANDY
The Pompidou.

FRANCES
Yeah! (beat) Um, do you get there?

RACHEL
They have a great place in the 6th.

ANDY
It’s a little pied a terre.

FRANCES
Literally!

ANDY
--but it’s a really special place.

JANELLE
It’s just been so hard to spend any time there since I got the job at the Journal. (beat) And the baby.

FRANCES
I’d love to go to Paris. I’ll bet it’s magic. One of my friends from college, Abby, who moved there because her boyfriend works for a bank. In school, she was on of the top five friends, but then Zoe became closer to our friend group and Abby kind of moved to the outer ring.

Everyone at the table listens politely. Rachel could give two shits.

FRANCES
Sophie and Abby never totally got along. She started dating Luke who I always thought would have dated any of us, but he ended up with Abby.

Silence.

ANDY
Well, let me know if you’re ever there, we like for it to be used.

FRANCES
Oh okay...I don’t see myself probably getting there super soon, but...thanks, thanks.
Frances feels hopeful, arriving at the airport in New York after impulsively purchasing a plane ticket to Paris.
Frances struggles to fall asleep on the long plane ride. Though sleep deprived, she still has high hopes for the trip.
A sleep deprived Frances arrives in Paris and struggles to navigate the city's transit system.
Frances arrives at the apartment, just as beautiful as promised.
As she looks out over the balcony, Frances feels excited for her solo weekend trip to explore the city, hoping to become as "cultured" as the rest.
Though exhausted, jetlag keeps Frances from falling asleep.
After falling asleep at 4 A.M., Frances sleeps in far longer than intended.
In hopes of saving what is left of the day, Frances exploress Paris's beautiful scenery.
Beginning to feel lonely, Frances calls her old friend Abby that moved to Paris. When she doesn't pick up, Frances leaves her an awkwardly desperate voicemail.
Frances continues walking through the city as the afternoon turns into evening, going to the city center to shop around.
Unfortunately, Frances slept away the day, and all shops are now closed.
Frances is turned away from the store, so she walks away contemplating what else to do with her time alone in the city.
Nothing seems to be going right during Frances trip– her cigarette won't even light.
While sitting alone and desperately bored at a cafe, Frances recieves a call from her estranged best friend.
Sophie is calling to invite Frances to her going away party that night. Frances had no idea she was moving to Japan, and doesn't tell her why she can't make it to her event. Nonetheless, the two reconcile over the phone.
Frances attempts to catch a last minute movie. The only one showing is Puss in Boots.
Another lonely walk around the city.
The next morning, Frances wakes up early after another night of fighting jet lag.
Frances's impulsive and short lived trip has come to an end. She flies back to New York.
In the taxi ride home (which will add even more debt to this trip) Frances listens to a voicemail left by Abby, who didn't get her message until a day after she had left it. She excitedly invites Frances to dinner that night in Paris.
